How the Local Community Can Help

The local community can play a powerful role in improving the park by actively participating in clean-up events, reducing their use of plastic when visiting the area, and supporting tree-planting or small garden-maintenance activities. Residents can also contribute by reporting issues directly through the digital platform so that broken equipment or waste accumulation can be addressed quickly. By staying mindful of how they dispose of trash and encouraging others to do the same, community members help create a cleaner, greener, and more welcoming park for everyone.

Eco-Friendly Actions

  • Visible garbage dumped in natural areas
  • Pollutes soil and water resources
  • Attracts pests and creates unpleasant odors
  • Reduces the aesthetic value of the park
  • Indicates lack of proper waste management behavior
  • Waste scattered near tree areas
  • Affects soil quality and plant growth
  • Creates hazards for small animals
  • Reduces biodiversity in the park
  • Shows a lack of awareness among visitors
  • Evidence of organized cleanup activities
  • Removes harmful waste from the environment
  • Encourages community participation
  • Improves the appearance and safety of the park
  • Supports long-term sustainability

How People Can Join Cleanup Events

Additional Ways People Can Join Cleanup Events

Join local cleanup events organized by communities or environmental groups.
Register or sign up in advance to help organizers prepare equipment.
Invite friends, family, or classmates to increase participation.
Follow safety rules and use gloves, trash bags, and proper clothing.
Share experiences and photos to encourage others to participate.

Long-Term Involvement and Community Impact

Regular cleanups help maintain a cleaner and healthier environment.
Community participation builds responsibility and awareness.
Encourages sustainable habits such as reducing waste and recycling.
Educates people about the importance of environmental protection.
Strengthens cooperation and unity within the community.
